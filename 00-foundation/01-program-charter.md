# 01 - AI Risk Program Charter

## 1. Purpose
This Charter establishes the AI Risk Program (the "Program") for <ORG>. The Program provides the governance, processes, and controls required to design, deploy, operate, and retire AI systems safely, lawfully, and ethically across all jurisdictions in which <ORG> operates, with dedicated overlays for the European Union and the United Arab Emirates.

## 2. Vision
Enable <ORG> to capture the upside of AI while keeping aggregate AI risk inside the Board-approved risk appetite. Build trust with customers, employees, regulators and the public by being demonstrably responsible by design.

## 3. Mission
Provide a single, enterprise-wide, risk-based framework that:
- Identifies every AI system in use, in build, or under procurement.
- Classifies each system by risk tier (Prohibited, High, Limited, Minimal) aligned to the EU AI Act and equivalent UAE expectations.
- Applies proportionate controls covering data quality, model robustness, security, transparency, human oversight, fairness, privacy, and accountability.
- Monitors AI systems throughout their lifecycle and reports residual risk to the Board.

## 4. Scope
In scope: any system, component, service, API, library, agent, or pipeline that uses machine learning, generative AI, foundation models, automated decision-making, computer vision, NLP, speech, or autonomous decision logic - whether built in-house, embedded in vendor products, or accessed via SaaS / API. Out of scope: deterministic rules engines with no learned parameters, classical statistics used for reporting only, and personal experimentation that never touches production data, customers, or employees.

## 5. Guiding principles
1. Lawful, ethical, robust - adopted from the EU High-Level Expert Group on AI.
2. Human-centric and human-accountable - every AI decision has a named owner.
3. Risk-proportionate - controls scale with impact and likelihood, not with hype.
4. Transparent - model cards, data cards and disclosure to affected persons.
5. Secure by design - AI is a new attack surface; treat it like one.
6. Privacy-preserving - minimisation, purpose limitation, DPIA where required.
7. Continuously monitored - drift, bias, performance and incident telemetry.
8. Auditable - every decision-eligible artefact is versioned and retained.

## 6. Authority
The Program is sponsored by the Board Risk Committee and chaired by the Chief AI Officer (or, where no CAIO exists, jointly by the Chief Risk Officer and Chief Information Security Officer). The Program has authority to:
- Block deployment of any AI system that fails risk acceptance.
- Mandate remediation, retraining, or retirement of any in-production AI system.
- Approve or veto third-party AI procurement above the materiality threshold.
- Set enterprise standards binding on all business units and subsidiaries.

## 7. Three lines of defence
- First line: AI product owners, data scientists, ML engineers, business sponsors. Own the risk; design and operate controls.
- Second line: AI Risk function, Model Risk Management, Compliance, Privacy, Security. Set standards, challenge, validate, and report.
- Third line: Internal Audit. Independent assurance over design and operating effectiveness.

## 8. Risk appetite (illustrative)
- Zero tolerance for prohibited AI practices (EU AI Act Article 5 equivalents).
- Zero tolerance for unapproved high-risk AI in production.
- Low tolerance for material bias, privacy breaches, or safety incidents.
- Moderate tolerance for experimental and sandboxed use cases with adequate guardrails.

## 9. Success measures
- 100 percent of AI systems inventoried within 6 months.
- 100 percent of high-risk systems with completed AI Impact Assessment before go-live.
- Mean time to detect AI incident below 24 hours; mean time to contain below 72 hours.
- Zero supervisory findings classified as material.

## 10. Review cadence
The Charter is reviewed annually by the Board Risk Committee and after any material change in regulation, business strategy, or risk profile.
