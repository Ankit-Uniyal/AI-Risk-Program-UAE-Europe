# 10 — Training and Awareness

The EU AI Act introduces a horizontal **AI literacy** obligation (Art. 4) that applies from 2 February 2025 to **providers and deployers** for staff and other persons operating AI on their behalf. UAE expectations under the National AI Strategy 2031 likewise prioritise capability-building. This document defines the curriculum and operating model.

## 1. Audience map

| Audience | Curriculum focus | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| All employees | AI literacy basics; Acceptable Use; GenAI hygiene; reporting concerns. | Onboarding + annual refresh. |
| AI product owners | Risk tiers; intake process; assessment artefacts; accountability. | At role assignment + annual. |
| Data scientists / ML engineers | Model cards, fairness, robustness, MLSecOps, privacy techniques. | Quarterly skills updates. |
| Reviewers / human-in-the-loop | Automation bias; how to override; appeal procedures; logging. | At role assignment + semi-annual. |
| Procurement | AI keyword detection; due-diligence questionnaire; clauses. | Annual. |
| Legal / Compliance / Privacy | EU AI Act / UAE PDPL / sector regs; supervisor expectations. | Continuous (CPD). |
| Security / SOC | AI threat landscape, OWASP LLM Top 10, MITRE ATLAS, red-team techniques. | Quarterly. |
| Executives and Board | Governance, risk appetite, scenario walk-throughs, peer benchmarks. | Semi-annual briefings. |

## 2. Curriculum modules

### M1 — AI literacy (60 minutes, all-staff)
- What AI is and isn't.
- How models can fail (bias, drift, hallucination, security).
- Our policies in plain language.
- Red flags and how to report.

### M2 — Acceptable use of GenAI (30 minutes, all-staff)
- Approved tools and tenancies.
- What you can and cannot paste into a prompt.
- Output verification expectations.
- Disclosure when reusing outputs.

### M3 — Building responsible AI (4 hours, builders)
- Lifecycle, controls, evidence.
- Bias, fairness, explainability hands-on.
- Threat modelling for AI.
- Hands-on labs with red-team tooling.

### M4 — Human oversight (90 minutes, reviewers)
- Automation bias studies and counter-measures.
- Override mechanics in our systems.
- Appeal handling and SLAs.

### M5 — AI for procurement (90 minutes)
- Walk-through of due-diligence questionnaire.
- Common vendor patterns and red flags.

### M6 — Board and Executive briefing (60 minutes)
- Regulatory state of play (EU AI Act, NIST, ISO 42001; UAE, CBUAE, DOH, DIFC).
- Top 10 enterprise AI risks for the next 12 months.
- Decisions ahead.

## 3. Delivery channels
- LMS for tracked completion and attestation.
- Lunch-and-learns and brown-bags.
- Internal AI risk wiki with searchable playbooks.
- Tabletop exercises (AI incident scenarios) for ARC and BRC.
- External certifications encouraged: ISO/IEC 42001 Lead Implementer, IAPP AIGP, NIST AI RMF Practitioner.

## 4. Evidence
- LMS completion records.
- Tabletop exercise reports.
- Knowledge-check pass rates.
- Reported incidents and near-misses (proxy for vigilance).

## 5. Culture interventions
- Senior-leader town-halls naming responsible-AI as a value.
- Internal "trust by design" awards.
- Public commitment to the UAE Charter principles where operating in the UAE; public AI principles for EU operations.
