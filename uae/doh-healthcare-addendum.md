# DOH (Abu Dhabi) — Healthcare AI Addendum

This addendum overlays expectations specific to the **Department of Health — Abu Dhabi (DOH)** and applies to healthcare providers, payers and digital-health vendors operating in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi. The anchor document is the **DOH Policy on Use of Artificial Intelligence in the Healthcare Sector of the Emirate of Abu Dhabi**.

## 1. Scope
Any AI system used in:
- Clinical decision support (diagnosis, prognosis, triage, treatment recommendation).
- Imaging and pathology interpretation.
- Predictive analytics on patient populations.
- Robotics, surgical assistance, devices with adaptive logic.
- Administrative automation that affects clinical workflows (scheduling, prior-authorisation, claims).
- Patient-facing chatbots, symptom checkers, and virtual care agents.

## 2. Pre-deployment expectations
- **Risk classification** of the AI system (typically Low / Moderate / High based on clinical impact and autonomy).
- **Clinical evidence** of safety and effectiveness appropriate to risk class — peer-reviewed validation, prospective testing in representative UAE populations where possible.
- **Regulatory clearance** where the AI is itself a medical device, including registration with DOH / MOHAP as applicable.
- **Data Protection Impact Assessment** covering patient data and any cross-border processing.
- **Cybersecurity assessment** aligned with DOH Information Security Policy.
- **Bias and representativeness analysis** with attention to UAE demographics (gender, nationality groups, age bands, comorbidities).

## 3. Human oversight
- A qualified, licensed clinician must remain accountable for every diagnostic and treatment decision informed by AI.
- AI is **decision-support**, not autonomous diagnosis, unless explicitly cleared.
- Override mechanisms must be designed-in and routinely exercised.
- Clinicians using AI must be trained in its capabilities and known failure modes.

## 4. Patient rights
- **Right to know** when AI contributes to care decisions.
- **Right to a non-AI alternative pathway** for material decisions, where practicable.
- **Right to a clinician's independent judgement**.
- **Right to data protection** and to know how the AI uses patient data, including training and reuse.

## 5. Data governance for clinical AI
- Patient data sovereignty: clinical data of UAE patients is sensitive; processing should remain UAE-resident or covered by robust safeguards.
- Provenance and lineage of training data documented.
- Synthetic data only used where validated to preserve clinical signal.
- De-identification standards documented and audited.

## 6. Post-market surveillance
- Continuous monitoring of clinical performance against pre-deployment benchmarks.
- Drift detection and triggered revalidation.
- Adverse-event reporting integrated with existing hospital incident systems.
- Periodic clinical-audit cycle including AI outcomes.

## 7. Generative AI in healthcare
- No autonomous diagnostic or therapeutic recommendation from generic LLMs to patients.
- Clinician-supervised summarisation, documentation and triage permitted with guardrails.
- Hallucination risk explicitly assessed and mitigated.
- Prompt logs retained per record-keeping rules.

## 8. Vendor management
- DOH vendor registration / approval where applicable.
- Contracts with model-change notification, data residency, audit rights, security obligations and indemnity for clinical harm.

## 9. Documentation evidencing compliance
- AIIA per system.
- Clinical-validation report.
- Cybersecurity assessment.
- DPIA.
- Training records for clinicians and operators.
- Post-market surveillance plan.
- Incident log.

## 10. Programme actions
- Appoint a **Clinical AI Lead** (typically a medical informatics consultant) on the ARC.
- Create a **Clinical AI Committee** that reviews every new clinical AI use case before approval.
- Integrate AI incidents with the Patient Safety Reporting system.
- Establish a periodic **clinical-AI audit** by Internal Audit jointly with Medical Affairs.
- For multi-emirate operations, align with DHA (Dubai) and MOHAP (federal) equivalents.
