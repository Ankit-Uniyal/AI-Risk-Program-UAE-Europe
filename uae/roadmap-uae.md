# UAE — Delivery Roadmap

A 12-to-18-month phased plan for UAE-operating entities, blending federal, emirate and free-zone requirements with the common foundation.

## Phase 0 — Mobilise (Month 0–1)
- Sponsor secured (CEO + CRO + CCO).
- Programme charter signed.
- ARC stood up with the right specialists: DPO, MLRO (financial), CMIO (healthcare), DIFC liaison.
- High-level UAE legal mapping done (which laws apply to which entities).
- Alignment with the **UAE Charter** principles publicly committed.

## Phase 1 — Foundations and quick wins (Month 1–3)
- Block prohibited use cases (manipulation, exploitation, biometric surveillance abuses) by policy and procurement gate.
- Enterprise-grade GenAI tenancy (UAE-resident where possible) provisioned.
- All-staff AI literacy training launched.
- Initial AI inventory (best-effort) captured.

## Phase 2 — Sector layering (Month 2–6)

### 2a. CBUAE-regulated entities
- Outsourcing register updated with all AI vendors.
- Model risk function stood up or augmented.
- AML / KYC / sanctions AI mapped into model inventory with validation schedule.
- Customer-fair-treatment KRIs added for AI-driven decisions.
- Engagement with CBUAE supervisors on novel use cases.

### 2b. Healthcare entities (DOH / DHA)
- Clinical AI Committee chartered.
- Clinical-evidence package required for every clinical AI use case.
- Patient disclosure UX implemented.
- Adverse-event reporting wired up.

### 2c. DIFC entities
- DIFC Autonomous Systems Register created.
- Reg. 10 risk assessments for in-scope systems.
- Human-review channel and SLA in product.
- Bias and accuracy monitoring operationalised.

## Phase 3 — Foundation deepening (Month 3–9)
- Full inventory (KRI-01 ≥ 98%).
- Policy stack approved.
- AIIA template piloted on 3 representative use cases per BU.
- Independent validation operating.
- Tooling decisions: governance plane, MLOps, observability, guardrails (with UAE data-residency preference).

## Phase 4 — Cross-jurisdiction alignment (Month 6–12)
- For entities with EU operations: align AIIA, DPIA and FRIA pipelines so a single intake produces multi-jurisdiction artefacts.
- For DIFC / ADGM entities: confirm Commissioner-of-DP-required notifications and registers.
- For multi-emirate healthcare entities: align DOH and DHA versions of documentation.
- Cross-border data transfer mechanisms documented and tested.

## Phase 5 — Assurance and certification (Month 9–18)
- Internal Audit thematic review of AI risk.
- ISO/IEC 42001 readiness assessment.
- Where strategic, pursue **ISO/IEC 42001 certification** (a recognised differentiator in UAE procurement).
- Engage UAE Charter publicly via reporting or attestation.

## Phase 6 — Continuous (ongoing)
- Quarterly Board reporting.
- Annual policy refresh.
- Tabletop exercises with regulators-style scenarios (CBUAE inspection, DOH audit, DIFC Commissioner query).
- Track new UAE AI guidance from the AI Office, sector regulators and federal initiatives, and roll changes into the control catalogue.

## Suggested early use-case priorities by sector
| Sector | Early use case | Why it is a useful pilot |
|---|---|---|
| Banking | Transaction-monitoring tuning AI | High visibility, validates MRM pipeline. |
| Insurance | Claims triage | Bias and explainability stress test. |
| Healthcare | Radiology decision-support | Tests clinical evidence and oversight pathway. |
| Government / public sector | Citizen-service chatbot | Tests transparency and accessibility. |
| Retail / e-commerce | Recommendation personalisation | Tests data-protection and minimisation. |
