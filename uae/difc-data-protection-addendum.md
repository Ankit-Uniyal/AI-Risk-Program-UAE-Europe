# DIFC — Data Protection Addendum (incl. Regulation 10 on Autonomous Systems)

This addendum applies to entities established or operating in the **Dubai International Financial Centre**. DIFC operates a common-law regime distinct from federal UAE law. The key instruments are:

- **DIFC Data Protection Law, DIFC Law No. 5 of 2020** (the "DPL").
- **DIFC Data Protection Regulations**, and specifically **Regulation 10 — The Processing of Personal Data through Autonomous and Semi-Autonomous Systems**.
- **DIFC Commissioner of Data Protection** — independent regulator.

DIFC's regime is closely aligned to GDPR, with explicit AI-relevant provisions in Regulation 10 — making it one of the more developed AI-data frameworks in the region.

## 1. Key concepts from the DPL relevant to AI
- Lawful bases (Art. 10) mirror GDPR Art. 6, including legitimate interests.
- Special categories of personal data (Art. 11) require enhanced safeguards.
- Rights of data subjects include access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection and rights regarding automated decisions.
- Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) required for high-risk processing (Art. 20).
- Privacy by design and by default (Art. 14).
- Accountability principle: controllers must demonstrate compliance.

## 2. Regulation 10 highlights (Autonomous and Semi-Autonomous Systems)
Regulation 10 specifically governs personal-data processing by **autonomous** systems (no meaningful human input in the relevant decision) and **semi-autonomous** systems (human input present but partial). Key obligations include:

- **Risk assessment**: a documented assessment of the system's risks to data subjects' rights and freedoms.
- **Transparency**: meaningful information about the logic involved and the significance of consequences for the data subject.
- **Human review**: a mechanism for data subjects to obtain meaningful human intervention.
- **Accuracy and bias**: measures to prevent inaccurate or discriminatory outcomes.
- **Record-keeping**: a register of autonomous systems used to process personal data, available to the Commissioner on request.
- **Ongoing monitoring** of system performance and impact on data subjects.

## 3. Programme actions for DIFC entities
1. Maintain a **DIFC Autonomous Systems Register** as a view of the master AI inventory, with required fields per Reg. 10.
2. Run a **Reg. 10 risk assessment** alongside the foundation AIIA (`../templates/ai-impact-assessment.md`) for every system processing personal data of DIFC data subjects.
3. Surface the **human-review pathway** in product UX (web, app, contact centre) — track requests and SLAs.
4. Embed **transparency notices** in customer journeys that explain logic and consequences in plain language.
5. **Bias monitoring** with metrics tailored to DIFC's diverse population and customer base.
6. Train the DPO and DP-team specifically on Reg. 10 expectations.
7. Maintain a **liaison with the DIFC Commissioner** — register, file required notifications, respond to Commissioner queries promptly.
8. For cross-border processing (e.g., training data sent outside DIFC), apply DIFC-approved transfer mechanisms.

## 4. Interaction with other DIFC laws
- **Operating Law and Regulations** for licensed entities (DFSA-regulated where in financial services) — DFSA technology and operational-risk expectations apply in parallel.
- **Cyber Security Law** (where issued by Dubai) for cyber resilience.
- DIFC-DFSA framework for **algorithmic and AI-driven trading** in financial markets.

## 5. Evidence pack for a Reg. 10 review
- Autonomous-systems register entry.
- Reg. 10 risk assessment.
- DPIA (where also required under Art. 20).
- Transparency notice copy and link to UX.
- Human-review log and SLA report.
- Bias and accuracy monitoring reports.
- Records of data-subject requests and outcomes.
- Vendor and sub-processor list with DPAs.
