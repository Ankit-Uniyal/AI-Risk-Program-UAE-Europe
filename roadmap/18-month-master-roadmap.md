# 18-Month Master Roadmap (Cross-Geography)

A single roll-up of foundation + Europe + UAE deliverables, by quarter, with primary owner.

## Q1 (Months 1–3) — Mobilise and quick wins
- Programme charter signed (CAIO / CRO).
- ARC / ARWG operational (Head of AI Risk).
- Prohibited-practice ban communicated and enforced via procurement (CPO + Head of AI Risk).
- Enterprise GenAI tenancy live with data-loss prevention (CISO).
- All-staff AI literacy training launched (HR + Head of AI Risk).
- High-level AI inventory baseline (Head of AI Risk).
- UAE Charter alignment statement published (CCO).

## Q2 (Months 4–6) — Foundation in place
- AI Risk Taxonomy and Methodology adopted.
- Control Catalogue mapped to EU AI Act, NIST, ISO 42001.
- Policy stack approved by ExCo (Policy 1–10).
- Inventory KRI-01 ≥ 90%.
- AIIA template piloted on 3 high-priority use cases per BU.
- Vendor due-diligence questionnaire integrated into procurement.
- GPAI register established (Europe).
- DIFC Autonomous Systems Register established (UAE/DIFC).
- Model Risk function stood up (CBUAE-regulated entities).

## Q3 (Months 7–9) — Sector and tooling
- Tooling decisions: governance plane, MLOps, observability, guardrails (CIO + CAIO).
- Clinical AI Committee live (DOH/DHA entities).
- CBUAE outsourcing register updated and notifications filed.
- DPIA / AIIA pipeline merged into a single intake.
- Independent validation operational.
- First independent red-team engagement completed for top-3 systems.
- Board reporting dashboard live (KRIs 01–20).

## Q4 (Months 10–12) — High-risk readiness
- Annex IV technical documentation drafted for every High-risk system.
- FRIA workflow live for relevant deployers.
- Post-market monitoring operational for all High-risk systems.
- Incident runbook tested via tabletop with executives (incl. regulator-style scenarios).
- Inventory KRI-01 ≥ 98%.
- Cross-jurisdiction artefact reuse demonstrated.
- ISO/IEC 42001 gap assessment completed.

## Q5 (Months 13–15) — Certify and assure
- ISO/IEC 42001 Stage 1 audit (if pursuing certification).
- Internal Audit AI thematic review concluded; findings tracked.
- DORA register fully integrated (financial entities, Europe).
- Patient-safety integration for clinical AI verified (healthcare entities).
- Three-year roadmap refresh signed by Board.

## Q6 (Months 16–18) — Steady state and continuous
- ISO/IEC 42001 Stage 2 audit and certification (if pursued).
- Regulatory horizon-scanning team operational (Compliance).
- KRIs in green for two consecutive quarters.
- Continuous-improvement backlog with ≥ 90% delivery against plan.
- Public responsible-AI report published (annual).

## Critical-path dependencies
1. **Foundation must be in place** before sector overlays — sequence Q1–Q2 before Q3 sectoral work.
2. **Tooling choice** must precede heavy AIIA volume; otherwise teams will reinvent.
3. **Inventory completeness** drives every metric — invest disproportionately in Q1–Q2.
4. **Board sponsorship** must be visible from day one or the programme stalls.

## Budget heuristic (illustrative, scale to your org)
- Year 1: 60% people (analysts, MLE, MRM, DPO uplift, training), 30% tooling, 10% external assurance.
- Year 2: 50% people, 30% tooling (full governance plane), 20% certification and audits.

## Common failure modes to avoid
- Treating AI risk as a project, not a programme — it never "ends".
- Buying a governance tool before defining the taxonomy and methodology.
- Letting "shadow GenAI" run unmanaged — discover, then govern.
- Confusing model risk with AI risk — the latter is broader and includes data, privacy, security, ethics, supply chain.
- Failing to involve Procurement, Legal, HR and Communications early.
